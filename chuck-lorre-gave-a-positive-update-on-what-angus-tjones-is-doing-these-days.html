Fans Are At Peace After Chuck Lorre Gave A Positive Update On What Angus T. Jones Is Doing These Day

Posted by Billy Koelling on Saturday, August 10, 2024

Highlights

  • Angus T. Jones is doing well and has moved on to projects outside of Hollywood, according to Chuck Lorre.
  • Fans were worried about Jones after his meltdown on Two and a Half Men, but he has since apologized and returned for the show's finale.
  • While Jones made a successful cameo on Bookie, Lorre isn't sure if Jon Cryer and Charlie Sheen will reunite in the future.

Fans will always speculate what Two and a Half Men could've been had Charlie Sheen stayed onboard for the duration. Nonetheless, the show enjoyed massive success, and Angus T. Jones was a major part of that. However, once he exited the show, the actor went off the map and fans haven't heard much since.

Fans were given a bit of an update recently thanks to Chuck Lorre. Jones made a cameo in Bookie. We'll reveal how that went, and what Lorre had to say about the child star's current status. In addition, we'll also take a closer look at Lorre's thoughts on Sheen possibly reuniting with Jon Cryer down the road.

Let's get started.

Chuck Lorre Said Angus T. Jones Is Doing "Great" With Projects Outside Of Hollywood

Since his days on Two and a Half Men, fans have gotten sporadic updates on Angus T. Jones through candid photos. However, when it comes to words from the actor himself, he hasn't really said much in the recent years. Speaking alongside Variety, Chuck Lorre discussed the reunion between Angus T. Jones and Charlie Sheen on Bookie.

While discussing the moment, Lorre revealed the actor's current status.

“He’s doing great,” Lorre said. “He’s a terrific guy. It’s an extraordinary thing. We started working together, he was eight years old. He’s a little boy, right? With just uncanny comedic instincts, without thinking. It was just in his DNA. And to work together again, 20 years later, we had fun. It was a wonderful afternoon. Everybody had the same reaction: ‘Oh my God. You’re a grown man!'”

As for what he's doing, Lorre didn't get into specifics, but revealed the former child star is working on things outside of Hollywood.

"Conversations with Angus would lead me to say no, he's done," he shared. "He has other goals in mind."

Fans Worried About Angus T. Jones Following His Meltdown On Two And A Half Men

He was the highest-paid child actor in the world. However, things completely changed after Angus went on a rant, going after Two and a Half Men and referring to the series as "filth."

He said, “If you watch Two and a Half Men, please stop watching Two and a Half Men. I’m on Two and a Half Men and I don’t want to be on it. Please stop watching it and filling your head with filth. People say it’s just entertainment. Do some research on the effects of television and your brain, and I promise you, you’ll have a decision to make when it comes to television, especially with what you watch.”

The meltdown caused lots of concerns, especially from his co-stars Ashton Kutcher and Jon Cryer. Soon after, Angus would issue an apology.

"I apologize if my remarks reflect me showing indifference to and disrespect of my colleagues and a lack of appreciation of the extraordinary opportunity of which I have been blessed. I never intended that.”

He continues, “I have been the subject of much discussion, speculation and commentary over the past 24 hours. While I cannot address everything that has been said or right every misstatement or misunderstanding, there is one thing I want to make clear. Without qualification, I am grateful to and have the highest regard and respect for all of the wonderful people on Two and Half Men with whom I have worked and over the past ten years who have become an extension of my family."

Thankfully, things ended off on the right note as the actor returned for the show's finale. He ended things off on good terms, but Lorre isn't sure about the same reality for Jon Cryer and Charlie Sheen.

Chuck Lorre Isn't So Sure That Charlie Sheen Will Also Reunite With Jon Cryer Down The Line

Angus T. Jones' return to Bookie was a success. However, Lorre doesn't feel as optimistic about a reunion between Jon Cryer and Charlie Sheen.

The TV creator said, "I don’t think so,” Lorre said. “Not that I’m aware of. It’s foolish to say never. It’s still to me more gratifying and fun to do new stuff. And to let let the stuff we did in the past be the past.”

As for Lorre and his own issues with Sheen, he's glad things are finally in the past.

“It was just a lot, for both of us, we were grateful to be past it,” he said of reuniting with Sheen. “Here we are, so many years later. And there was a lot of gratitude. I think it was warm. People said it must have been healing and you know, it was. It was wonderful to not just give the man a hug. We worked together and we had a blast. It was very reminiscent of those wonderful eight years where we had a lot of fun making a show that we worked hard on and we were proud of.”
