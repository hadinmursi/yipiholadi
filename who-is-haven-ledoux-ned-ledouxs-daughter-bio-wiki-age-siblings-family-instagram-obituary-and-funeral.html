Haven LeDoux Bio – Haven LeDoux Wiki

Haven LeDoux is Ned LeDoux’s daughter with his wife, Morgan, and Chris LeDoux’s grandaughter.

Haven LeDoux Age

She was 2 years old. On September 15, Ned celebrated his daughter’s birthday in an Instagram post. He captioned the post, “Turned the big #2 today! Happy to be home for this special day. What a character this one.”

Haven LeDoux Death

Haven died on October 20, 2019 at the family home in northeast Kansas. The sad news was confirmed on October 27 by a representative for the country singer on Ned LeDoux’s Facebook page. The Facebook post said: “It is with great sadness that Ned & Morgan inform friends and fans that their two year old daughter Haven passed away on October 20th due to a tragic choking accident in the home. The LeDoux family appreciates your love and support and is requesting privacy at this time.”

Kendra Santos wrote on Facebook, “Prayers for peace in the hearts of the LeDoux family, as they suffer the unthinkable sadness of the sudden loss of little Haven, the 2-year-old daughter of Ned and Morgan and the granddaughter of Chris and Peggy. While I have no doubt that Papa Chris was there to meet their sweet angel with open arms, there could be no greater sadness for those who will miss her most until they meet again. Love to all LeDouxs from your rodeo family. ♥️??♥️”

Haven LeDoux Cause of Death – How Did Haven LeDoux Die

Haven LeDoux’s cause of death was a choking accident.

Ned LeDoux’s Daughter, Children

In addition to his daughter, Haven LeDoux, he has a son named Bronson. In a 2018 interview with Cavvy Savvy, Ned LeDoux said of his family, “My wife and I have been married for 10 years this May. I’m a lucky man. We have 2 beautiful kiddos. Bronson 7 and Haven 6 months. We now live in northeast Kansas. I still do a little ranch work when we’re back at Mom’s. But music has always been the dream. I’m very very grateful to be able to live that dream.”

Ned LeDoux Family

Ned LeDoux is the son of Chris LeDoux, the famed bareback rider and country singer. Chris LeDoux died after a battle with cancer in March 2005 at the age of 56.
